AP European History
Resources

Unit - 1

This unit examines the cultural, political, and economic transformations that marked the transition from the medieval world to early modern Europe. Students explore Renaissance humanism, artistic innovation, and the growth of centralized states, as well as the motivations and consequences of European exploration and global interaction.

Unit - 3

This unit focuses on the development of different systems of government in Europe following the Thirty Years War. Students compare absolutist and constitutional states, examine political theory, and analyze how rulers used power to maintain control and respond to social and economic challenges.

Unit-3 Notes
Unit -3 Review Videos

Unit - 5

This unit analyzes the causes and consequences of major political revolutions, especially the French Revolution and the Napoleonic era. Students evaluate how revolutionary ideals challenged traditional structures and how European states responded to the resulting social and political instability.

Unit-5 Notes
Unit -5 Review Videos

Unit - 7

This unit examines nationalism, liberalism, conservatism, and socialism as forces shaping European politics and culture. Students analyze the unification of Italy and Germany, the decline of multinational empires, and debates over citizenship, rights, and identity.

Unit-7 Notes
Unit -7 Review Videos

Unit - 2

Students study the religious upheavals of the Protestant and Catholic Reformations and their far-reaching effects on European society and politics. The unit emphasizes religious conflict, the rise of religious pluralism, and the role of the state in enforcing or resisting religious change.

Unit-2 Notes
Unit -2 Review Videos

Unit - 4

Students explore the intellectual movements that reshaped European thought, including the Scientific Revolution and the Enlightenment. The unit also examines how new ideas influenced political reforms, revolutions, and changing views of authority, rights, and governance.

Unit-4 Notes
Unit -4 Review Videos

Unit - 6

Students investigate the origins and spread of industrialization and its impact on European economies and societies. The unit highlights changes in labor, urbanization, social class, and government responses to industrial challenges, including reform movements and new ideologies.

Unit-6 Notes
Unit -6 Review Videos

Unit - 8

Students study the causes, conduct, and consequences of World War I and World War II. The unit emphasizes total war, mass mobilization, ideological conflict, and the profound political, social, and economic changes that reshaped Europe in the 20th century.

Unit-8 Notes
Unit -8 Review Videos

Unit - 9

This unit explores Europes recovery from World War II, the division and later reunification of the continent, and the challenges of the Cold War. Students also examine decolonization, European integration, shifting social identities, and the political and economic developments shaping modern Europe.
